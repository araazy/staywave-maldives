Fuvahmulah Dive Sites

Explore the legendary deep channels, tiger shark encounters, and vibrant reef walls of Fuvahmulah.

Dive Site Guide Overview

Fuvahmulah is known for pelagic action and unique topography. This guide highlights key sites for experienced divers, with depth profiles, marine life expectations, and planning notes based on local conditions.

Featured Dive Sites

Tiger Zoo

Type: Deep channel / tiger shark point

Typical Depth: 20–40m

Highlights: Tiger sharks, occasional silvertips, strong current action.

Farikede

Type: Reef wall / drift

Typical Depth: 10–30m

Highlights: Grey reef sharks, schooling jacks, healthy coral growth.

Thoondu Corner

Type: Corner drift

Typical Depth: 15–35m

Highlights: Big pelagics, eagle rays, fast drift windows.

Dive-Site Map Notes

Use the dive-site map to orient north/south drop points, identify preferred current entries, and plan alternate pickups based on sea state.

  • Primary drop zones are selected by prevailing current direction.
  • Southern edges often provide better pelagic sightings.
  • Always coordinate with local guides for same-day safety checks.
