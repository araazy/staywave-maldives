Underwater Photography Tips

1. Camera Setup

  • Full-frame or crop sensor cameras
  • Action cameras for beginners
  • Ensure proper maintenance after each dive

2. Recommended Lenses

  • Wide-angle lens for sharks and manta rays
  • Fisheye lens for close encounters
  • Dome port for split-level photography

3. Best Practices

  • Maintain neutral buoyancy
  • Avoid chasing marine life
  • Respect wildlife behavior
  • Keep a safe distance

4. Camera Care

  • Rinse equipment with fresh water
  • Check O-rings before every dive
  • Dry equipment thoroughly before storage

Responsible Diving

Protecting Fuvahmulah's ecosystem is part of every Staywave Maldives dive. Our briefings follow a low-impact approach designed to keep divers and wildlife safe.

  • Maintain neutral buoyancy to avoid reef contact and sediment disturbance.
  • Never touch, feed, or chase sharks, rays, turtles, or baitfish schools.
  • Use reef-safe sunscreen and secure loose gear before entry.
  • Follow your guide's depth, current, and spacing instructions at all times.
  • Keep camera etiquette in mind: no blocking exits, no crowding animals.

Frequently Asked Questions

What certification level do I need for marine life dives in Fuvahmulah?

Many pelagic dives are best for Advanced Open Water divers or equivalent, with recent current-diving experience.

Can beginners still join marine life trips?

Yes. We can recommend suitable sites and training dives first, then progress based on your comfort and conditions.

When is the best time to see tiger sharks?

Tiger sharks can be seen year-round, with consistently strong encounters from December through May.

Do you guarantee sightings of specific species?

No operator can guarantee wildlife sightings. We plan around seasons and local conditions to maximize your chances.
