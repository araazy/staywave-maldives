Cancellation Policy

A clear overview of how guest cancellations, date changes, supplier conditions, and travel disruption may affect a planned holiday.

Read the Policy Start Planning

Clear Expectations Around Changes

Travel plans can shift, so it helps to understand how cancellation and change decisions may affect a holiday before anything is confirmed.

Because Fuvahmulah trips can involve accommodation, domestic flights, and dive services from third-party providers, cancellation outcomes are often influenced by supplier terms as well as timing. The earlier a change is raised, the better the chance of retaining flexibility.

This policy is intended to explain the practical approach StayWave Maldives takes when helping guests understand cancellations, date changes, and disruption scenarios connected to a planned trip.

  • Timing mattersEarlier notice generally creates more room for practical solutions or reduced supplier penalties.
  • Third-party rules applyFlights, accommodation, and dive operators may each have their own cancellation conditions.
  • Insurance helpsTravel insurance can be important protection for events outside your control.

Guest Cancellations and Date Changes

These are the most common scenarios guests ask about during planning.

Before Confirmation

If you decide not to proceed before any services are confirmed, there is usually far more flexibility because supplier commitments may not yet exist.

After Confirmation

Once services are confirmed, cancellation terms may depend on notice period, supplier rules, and any non-refundable costs already committed.

Date Change Requests

Moving a trip to new dates is sometimes possible, but availability and revised pricing may differ from the original plan.

Operational Disruption and Supplier Conditions

Not every change originates with the guest, and some issues depend on external conditions.

Weather and Sea Conditions

Dive schedules or holiday components may need adjustment when safety, weather, or marine conditions make the original plan unsuitable.

Transport Disruption

Domestic and international travel delays, airline changes, or baggage issues can affect arrival plans and may require itinerary adjustments.

Supplier Policies

Accommodation and dive partners may set their own refund, amendment, or credit rules, which can influence what is possible in a given case.

Refunds, Credits, and Planning Advice

A measured approach helps guests understand realistic outcomes before they commit.

Refund Outcomes Vary

Refund eligibility depends on timing, what has already been confirmed, and which supplier conditions apply to the affected service.

Credits May Be Possible

In some situations, a credit or rebooking option may be more practical than a direct refund, depending on partner terms and availability.

Written Confirmation Helps

Keep travel confirmations and change requests documented so there is a clear record of timing and agreed next steps.

Insurance Is Recommended

A policy that covers cancellations, delays, and diving-related travel issues can provide important financial protection.

Best Practice for Guests

These steps help reduce confusion if plans change.

Raise Changes Early

Contact StayWave Maldives as soon as you think a change may be needed so options can be checked before they narrow.

Share Full Details

Provide names, dates, flight information, and the reason for the change so the relevant suppliers can be consulted efficiently.

Review Supplier Terms

Where an arrangement depends on third-party services, their rules may need to be reviewed alongside this policy.

Expect Practical Guidance

We aim to explain realistic options clearly and help guests understand the implications before deciding how to proceed.
